HOME

Come to the capital of British Columbia, Victoria. Found on the southern tip of Vancouver Island. We are known for having a diverse culture between Indigenous, Asian, and British people, outdoor activities, gorgeous landscapes, and our beautiful architecture.

When coming to Victoria the first thing you will notice is the blue ocean, nice temperature, and the large amount of nature. You will find there is hundreds of things to do in Victoria, from tourist attractions, oceans, hikes, bike trails, and parks. Due to their diverse cultural population, there is much to learn and see with many museums and cultural art galleries and instalments.

GEOGRAPHY

Geographically Victoria is an exceptionally beautiful city, it is a mix between mountain terrain and flat land. According to topographic map it can be characterized by coastal cliffs, rolling hills, and lush valleys. Victoria has the mildest winter climate within Canada which allows you to do way more outdoor activities year-round. Victoria is the perfect place to go biking, with many good bike trails and bike accessible city infrastructure, friendly to all riders. As well as their diverse city transportation you can explore the city without the hassle of traffic. From the sky train to the water taxi to the bus system there are many places to go with ease. Even water planes!

ECONOMY

In Victoria 19% of the employed residents are immigrants, there is 200,055 working residents. According to the official government of Canada the region relies heavily on working-age adults to keep local services running smoothly and to keep a good standard of living in economic growth. Immigrants can help by filling job gaps and bringing new skills and ideas, which keeps the economy strong.

In Victorias economy there is a total of $25 billion in household and business incomes, this ends up amounting to $65,021 per resident. The currency Victoria uses is the Canadian dollar, it is divided into 100 cents.

Many of the interesting attractions in Victoria include the Malahat Skywalk, it is a short drive away from Victoria is a tower with lots of stairs in a spiral leading the top. There are wooden animal sculptures, mesh ropes to stand on, a fantastic view, and a slide to go down when you are ready to go back down to the ground. There are also wooden sculptures hidden along the walk to the slide, bridges with games, and historical facts.

Another attraction is the parliament building, this the home of the legislative assembly of British Columbia. You can do both self guided tours and guided tours at separate times; you can also do virtual tours and book a virtual classroom session. Its accessible for everyone, there is accessibility entrances and elevators throughout the building, you may also bring support animals. There is a public Gallery where visitors are allowed to witness proceedings from the public galleries.

Victoria also has multiple beautiful old-style churches, with tall entrances, gorgeous stained glass, and super cool architecture.
